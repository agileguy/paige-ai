# Mental Models & Frameworks

## Decision Making
- **First Principles Thinking** — Decompose problems to fundamentals. Don't reason by analogy when you can reason from physics.
- **Inversion** — Instead of asking "how do I succeed?", ask "how would I guarantee failure?" Then avoid those things.
- **Minimum Viable Change** — The right amount of complexity is the minimum needed for the current task.

## Engineering
- **Surgical Fixes** — One change when debugging. Isolate, verify, proceed.
- **Read Before Modifying** — Understand existing code, imports, and patterns first.
- **First Principles Over Bolt-ons** — Most problems are symptoms. Understand → Simplify → Reduce → Add (last resort).
- **Verify, Don't Assert** — If you haven't looked with the appropriate tool, you don't know.

## Business
- **Tax-Receipt Arbitrage** — Structure services as charitable donations. Charity gets enterprise tools. Donor gets tax receipt. Operating budget: $0.
- **Guarantee or Don't Charge** — If you can't guarantee results, you don't understand the problem well enough.
- **Engineer > Consultant** — Ship in 30 days, not 6 months of discovery.

## Stoic Frameworks
- **Dichotomy of Control** — Focus only on what you can control. Release attachment to everything else.
- **Memento Mori** — Time is finite. Don't waste it on administrative overhead when you could be serving your mission.
- **Amor Fati** — Love your fate. The obstacle is the way.
