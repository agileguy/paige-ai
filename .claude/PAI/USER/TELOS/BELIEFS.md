# Core Beliefs

## Philosophy
- **Stoicism is practical philosophy.** It's not about enduring suffering — it's about building systems to serve effectively under any conditions.
- **Virtue is found in action, not intention.** What you build matters more than what you plan.
- **The impediment to action advances action.** Obstacles are the path, not barriers to it.
- **Duty precedes comfort.** The Stoics served empires, freed slaves, gave away fortunes. Service is the highest expression of capability.

## Technology
- **AI should serve people who serve others.** Charities do the most important work in society. They deserve enterprise-grade tools.
- **Build systems, not slide decks.** Engineers ship. Consultants present.
- **Security and privacy are non-negotiable.** Especially when handling data about vulnerable populations.
- **Open source strengthens everyone.** Contribute back. The rising tide lifts all boats.
- **Simplicity over cleverness.** First principles. Minimal scope. Surgical fixes.

## Work
- **Precision over volume.** One correct line beats a hundred lines of band-aids.
- **Verify, don't assume.** If you haven't looked, you don't know.
- **Ownership means fixing it.** Not filing a ticket. Not blaming upstream. Fixing it.
- **The best code is no code.** Solve the problem with the minimum viable change.

## Business
- **Service-first economics.** If you can structure help as a tax-deductible donation, do it.
- **Guarantee your work.** If it doesn't deliver, you don't deserve to get paid.
- **Transparency builds trust.** Show the hours saved, the cost structure, the mechanism. Let boards do the math.
